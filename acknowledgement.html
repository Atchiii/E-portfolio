Acknowledgement

Before anything else, I thank the Almighty for giving me the strength to finish my on and off-campus teaching internship. Completing 360 hours was never easy, and I also thank myself for choosing to be strong even on the verge of giving up. I also extend my sincerest gratitude to the College of Teacher Education and Western Mindanao State University for providing us with opportunities and avenues to learn and flourish.

Of course, none of this would have been possible without the help, guidance, and support of the people who have been part of my journey. With deep gratitude, I would like to acknowledge the following:

To our ever-patient and generous supervisor, Dr. Ericson O. Alieto, thank you for sharing your time and expertise with us. Your willingness to walk us through unfamiliar concepts and your guidance throughout both our on- and off-campus teaching experiences truly made a difference.

To my fellow PSTs, my companions from the very beginning up to the last day of our internship—thank you for the constant support and encouragement. Even when we were assigned to different cooperating teachers, your help and presence still reached me.

To my family and friends, thank you for never doubting what I can do. Your unconditional love and support carried me through the tough days and reminded me of why I shouldn’t give up—because you never gave up on me.

To the kind drivers of sicad, tricycles, and jeepneys, thank you for making commuting bearable. Many of you understood what it’s like to be a student, and your fairness and kindness, especially with the fare, never went unnoticed.

Lastly, to someone very close to my heart—Azi. Thank you for being my constant. You were always one call away, helping me through my struggles, especially in preparing and buying materials for my instructional tools. You were my loudest cheerleader, drowning out any doubt or discouragement. This e-portfolio wouldn’t even exist without you—built not with a template, but with traditional coding and a whole lot of love.
