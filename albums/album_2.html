Experiences On-Campus Off-campus

Week 2

In the second week, our mentor assigned us topics to teach. For me, the focus was on quoting, paraphrasing, and summarizing, which are crucial skills for both reading and writing. I researched and reviewed these concepts, wanting to make sure I had a clear grasp before preparing my lessons. I then began developing my lesson plans, ensuring that each one aligned with the learning objectives and addressed the needs of my students.

Along with lesson planning, I dedicated time to creating instructional materials. I designed PowerPoint presentations, printed worksheets, and crafted visual aids, including an incentive chart and interactive materials. The process was challenging at times, especially when I found myself staying up late to finish, but I enjoyed every bit of it. Since my students were in Grade 7, I knew I had to simplify the content, treating them like Grade 6 students to ensure they could grasp the material more easily.

By the end of the week, my lesson plans were reviewed by our mentor. He provided feedback, suggesting that I divide the topics into two separate lessons. Although the thought of our supervisor observing my class made me nervous, I was also excited to finally put my preparation into action.
