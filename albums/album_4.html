Experiences On-Campus Off-campus

Week 4

In our final week, we shifted focus to grading and recording the students' performance. We checked their worksheets, participation, and written outputs from previous lessons, carefully documenting their progress. It was rewarding to see how much the students had grown and learned over the past weeks. We paid close attention to detail, ensuring that the grading was accurate and fair. Alongside the grading, we also worked on organizing and encoding documents as part of our tasks under our mentors.

Saying goodbye was difficult, but it was a beautiful way to end our on-campus teaching experience. I felt grateful for the bonds I had formed with my students, and the entire experience left a lasting impression on me. I also took a moment to thank my mentor for their guidance throughout the practicum. The week was a reminder that teaching is not just about delivering lessons but also about the relationships we build with our students. I ended the journey with a heart full of gratitude and a renewed passion for the teaching profession.
