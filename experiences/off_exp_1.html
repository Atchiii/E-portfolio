Experiences On-Campus Off-campus

Reflection 1: Content, Knowledge and Pedagogy

Teaching Grade 9 English at Zamboanga National High School West was both challenging and rewarding. I covered topics like the relevance of one's ideas, factual versus subjective information, and inferencing. To ensure I was well-prepared, I delved into each subject thoroughly before stepping into the classroom. I carefully chose examples and designed activities that aligned with the lesson objectives and suited the students' comprehension levels, always keeping the curriculum guide in mind. My goal was to make each lesson meaningful and clear.

This is the description for Photo 1.

I paid close attention to the students' reactions during lessons. Whenever I noticed confusion or hesitation, I would rephrase my explanations or employ different teaching methods to clarify the points. Additionally, incorporating collaborative tasks allowed students to express their thoughts and reinforced their understanding more effectively, and utilizing visual aids and real-life examples made the lessons more relatable and boosted student engagement.

This is the description for Photo 2.

From this experience, I realized the importance of combining strong content knowledge with effective pedagogical strategies. Planning and delivering lessons in a new and unfamiliar environment was challenging, but it helped me become more confident in making quick decisions and adapting to classroom dynamics. This off-campus experience enhanced both my teaching content and delivery, teaching me to create more student-centered and adaptable lessons.
