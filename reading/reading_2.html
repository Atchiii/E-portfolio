Reflection 2: Basic Education

According to the EDCOM II report, Basic Education is at the heart of the crisis. Issues like overcrowded classrooms, lack of instructional time, outdated learning materials, and teacher shortages are evident. What makes it more problematic is that many students are not mastering the basics like reading, writing, and math, which seriously threatens their future opportunities. Also, the report stresses how instructions and new implementations become difficult because of the highly centralized structure of DepEd, which becomes a challenge. Although the revised curriculum may enhance student learning, it still becomes limited because teachers could not implement it effectively. Nonetheless, efforts are being made to fix the educational system, and it has also been highlighted in the report how important it is to go back to foundational skills and making sure no learner is left behind.

As a pre-service teacher who spent time both on- and off-campus, I witnessed these challenges up close. I saw how difficult it is for teachers to manage big classes with limited resources. I also experienced the struggle of keeping students engaged when some were still catching up on skills they should’ve learned in earlier grades. There were moments when I had to pause and reteach basic concepts because I couldn’t move forward otherwise.

The reports in basic education resonated with what I saw and experienced firsthand during my internship. This made me realize that teaching is not just about delivering lessons. It is about bridging gaps in the learning environment, resources, and in honing the skills of the learners while making sure that there will be no one left behind the process of learning.
