Weekly Journal (Off-campus)

Week 1

My first week at Zamboanga National High School West was a mix of excitement and nervousness as I adjusted to the new environment, met my mentor, and observed classes. Though overwhelmed, I connected with some students and felt more at ease by the end of the week.

Week 2

In my second week, I taught my first lesson on the relevance of one’s idea. Despite some nervousness and varying student responses, I adapted my approach, used visual aids, and reflected on my performance, learning that teaching requires continuous adjustment.

Week 3

I taught about "factual and subjective information" in Week 3, facing challenges with classroom management and varying student comprehension. Despite struggles, I adapted on the spot, used visual aids, and became more confident in handling the class.

Week 4

Week 4 was nerve-wracking as I taught inferencing with my supervisor observing. Although the lesson didn’t go as smoothly as I’d hoped, the surprise of heartfelt letters and gifts from my students reminded me of the impact I had, reaffirming my passion for teaching.
